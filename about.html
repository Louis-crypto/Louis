About us

Primary Schools

We meet drama most frequently in the theatre, on television or in the cinema, and we associate it with performance, costumes , setting and stages. Similarly, in school we also associate drama with script-writing, rehearsals, voice production and the display of acting talent. This type of drama has certain benefits to pupils d in that it increases children’s self-confidence, gives them the opportunity to express themselves in public and allows them the opportunity of appearing on stage.

Aims

The aims of the drama curriculum are;
  1. to enable the child to become drama literate
  2. to enable the child to create a permanent bridge between play and the art form of theatre
  3. to develop the child’s ability to enter physically, emotionally and intellectually into the drama world in order to promote questing, empowering and empathetic skills
  4. to enable the child to develop the social skills necessary to engage, openly, honestly and playfully with others
  5. to enable the child to co-operate and communicate with others in solving problems in the drama and through the drama

Secondary Schools

In secondary schools drama is used as a form of learning languages, as we know language comes in different types and form; ie. poetry, drama, legends, myths etc. Drama helps students be able to visualise the language they learn and its reality hence helps them to understand better what they are taught in school. Drama is also an outdoor activity and it promotes socializing among people from different backgrounds, hence promoting unity and tolerance among students from an early age. However they are some hitches the Kenya National Drama and Film Festival is trying to solve to ensure te whole festival is a fun experience and learning opportunity.

Aims

The aims of Drama in Secondary schools are;
  1. Promote tolerance among students from different backgrounds
  2. Promote learning through visual arts
  3. Promote self presentation skills among students and help them to express themselves fluently
  4. Promote cultural diversity

College and University(Tertiary)

Drama in college and university is advanced in that it now has more of film than play so as to develop talent among the youth and also so that the talented youth can learn how the kenyan film industry works, qualities and ethics that are expected of them. It majorly focuses on employing youth, promotes networking as youth studying different courses are able to meet and socialize and this increases their network hence they are able to provide opportunities for each other. Drama and Film at tertiary level also encourages learning of different cultures which promotes diversity among Kenyans hence borrowing of practices among people from different backgrounds which also promotes tolerance.

  1. Promotes networking among youth
  2. To be a source of employment
  3. Promotes tolerance of different cultures
  4. Encourages cultural diversity
